EVIDENCE DOCUMENT #2

Witness Statements

Case #2024-HC-0847

Statement #1: Marcus Chen
Position: Assistant Curator
Date: December 16, 2024, 9:30 AM
Interviewed by: Detective Sarah Morrison
Q: What time did you arrive at the museum on December 15th?
A: I came in around 9:00 AM as usual. It was a normal workday until Dr. Sterling called me into her office around 3 PM.
Q: What did she want to discuss?
A: She seemed upset. She accused me of questioning her authentication of the Midnight Diamond. I had mentioned to a colleague that I thought we should get a second opinion, but it was just professional caution. She took it as an attack on her expertise.
Q: Then what happened?
A: She asked me to stay late. Said we needed to talk more about it. I got an email at 7:15 PM confirming the meeting at 8:30. When I went to her office at 8:30, her door was closed and locked. I knocked several times but got no answer. I assumed she changed her mind and went home.
Q: What time did you leave the museum?
A: I stayed in my office working on exhibition catalogs until about 9:30 PM, then I left. Look, I know the parking records show my car leaving later, but I can explain that. I forgot my phone in my office and had to come back around 10:45 PM. I was only inside for about 15 minutes.
Q: Did you see anyone else during your second visit?
A: No, the place was quiet. I thought I heard voices from the direction of Gallery Wing 3, but the building echoes a lot at night. Could have been the security guard's radio.
Q: Do you have any reason to believe Dr. Sterling was planning to steal the diamond?
A: No! She loved that museum. But... she had been stressed lately. Money troubles, I think. And she kept getting these calls that would make her upset. I don't want to speak ill of her, but something was definitely wrong.
Statement #2: Elena Rodriguez
Position: Night Security Guard
Date: December 16, 2024, 2:15 PM
Interviewed by: Detective Sarah Morrison
Q: Walk me through your shift on December 15th.
A: I started at 7:45 PM. Standard procedure—I check all the doors, make sure the alarm system is armed properly, then do rounds every hour. Everything was normal that night.
Q: Did you see Dr. Sterling?
A: On the cameras, yes. I saw her enter her office around 8:47 PM. She looked agitated, kept looking over her shoulder. I made a note of it in my log because it seemed unusual.
Q: What about other staff members?
A: I saw Marcus Chen leave around 9:30, then that art dealer Wellington left around 10:45. I didn't know Wellington was in the building—there was no visitor sign-in. That worried me, so I did an extra round of Gallery Wing 3 at 11:00 PM.
Q: And what did you find?
A: The Midnight Diamond display case was closed and locked, everything looked fine. But when the alarm went off at 11:23, I ran back and the diamond was gone. The case was wide open.
Q: How is that possible if you just checked it 23 minutes earlier?
A: I don't know! Only Dr. Sterling and I had keys to that case. My key was on me the whole time. She must have come back after I did my round.
Q: We need to ask about your financial situation.
A: I know how it looks. Yes, I have debts. Yes, I asked Dr. Sterling for help. But I would never steal from this museum. It's been my life for 15 years. I'm telling you, check the cameras again. Someone else was there after my 11:00 round.
Statement #3: James Wellington
Position: Private Art Dealer
Date: December 17, 2024, 10:00 AM
Interviewed by: Detective Sarah Morrison
Q: Why were you at the museum on December 15th?
A: I had a 7:00 PM appointment with Dr. Sterling. We were discussing a potential acquisition for the museum—a 17th-century Persian manuscript. It's what I do for a living, Detective.
Q: But you've made multiple offers to buy the Midnight Diamond.
A: On behalf of a client, yes. That's different from theft. My client is a legitimate collector who's willing to pay far above market value. Dr. Sterling refused every offer, which is her right.
Q: What time did your meeting end?
A: Around 8:30 PM. The meeting was brief—she wasn't interested in the manuscript. She seemed distracted, kept checking her phone. When I mentioned the diamond one more time, she got angry and asked me to leave.
Q: Why didn't you sign out at the visitor log?
A: Dr. Sterling met me at a side entrance. Said it would be more convenient. I thought it was odd, but I didn't question it.
Q: Security cameras show your car leaving at 10:45 PM. Where were you for those two hours?
A: I was at a nearby coffee shop working on my laptop. I have a receipt. After our unpleasant meeting, I needed to clear my head before the drive home.
Q: Did you see anything unusual before you left?
A: Actually, yes. When I was walking to my car around 8:40 PM, I saw someone—a woman—walking quickly toward the back of the museum. She was wearing a hood, so I couldn't see her face clearly. At the time, I assumed it was another staff member.
Statement #4: Robert Hayes
Position: Evening Janitor
Date: December 16, 2024, 4:00 PM
Interviewed by: Detective Sarah Morrison
Q: What were you doing on December 15th?
A: Normal evening shift. I clean the administrative offices and public areas after the museum closes. Start at 6:30, usually done by 10:00 PM.
Q: You reported hearing raised voices. Tell me about that.
A: Yeah, around 8:30 PM. I was cleaning the hallway outside Dr. Sterling's office. I heard her arguing with someone—sounded like a man. They were shouting about "the plan" and "too risky." I couldn't make out everything, but Dr. Sterling said something like "I won't be a part of this anymore."
Q: Did you see who was with her?
A: No, her door was closed. But about five minutes later, a man came out—that art dealer, Wellington. He looked angry, face all red. He practically ran past me without saying a word.
Q: What did Dr. Sterling do after that?
A: She came out maybe 15 minutes later, around 8:47. She looked upset, like she'd been crying. She went toward the gallery wing. I finished my work and left around 9:15 PM.
Q: Did you see anyone else that evening?
A: Marcus Chen was still in his office when I left. And I saw that board member, Dr. Moore, leaving through the back exit around 8:00 PM. Thought that was strange since board members usually use the front entrance.
